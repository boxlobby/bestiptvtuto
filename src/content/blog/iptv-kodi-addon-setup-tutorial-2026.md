## What You'll Need for IPTV on Kodi (2026)

Setting up IPTV on Kodi in 2026 is relatively straightforward, but having the right components is crucial for a smooth experience. Here's a breakdown of what you'll need:

### Hardware & Software Requirements

1.  **A Compatible Device:** Kodi is highly versatile and runs on many platforms. You'll need a device capable of running Kodi, such as a smart TV (Android-based), a dedicated streaming box (like NVIDIA Shield, Amazon Fire TV Stick), a computer (Windows, macOS, Linux), or even a smartphone/tablet. Ensure your device meets the minimum processing power and memory requirements for Kodi, especially if you plan on streaming high-definition or 4K content.

2.  **Stable Internet Connection:** IPTV relies heavily on a consistent and fast internet connection. A broadband connection with a minimum download speed of 25 Mbps is recommended for reliable HD streaming. For 4K content, aim for 50 Mbps or higher. Consider a wired Ethernet connection for optimal stability, although a strong Wi-Fi signal can suffice.

3.  **Kodi Software:** Download and install the latest stable version of Kodi from the official Kodi website or your device's app store. Make sure you choose the correct version for your operating system. Older versions might not be compatible with newer IPTV add-ons or streaming protocols.

4.  **IPTV Service Subscription:** You'll need a subscription to a reputable IPTV service provider. Research and choose a provider that offers the channels and content you want and that supports the m3u playlist format or other compatible formats. Consider factors like price, channel selection, server stability, and customer support.

5.  **IPTV Add-on for Kodi:** In 2026, several Kodi add-ons will be available for handling IPTV streams. Popular choices may include PVR IPTV Simple Client (often pre-installed with Kodi) or other third-party add-ons designed for specific IPTV providers or functionalities. Research and install a well-regarded add-on that is compatible with your IPTV service.

## Installing an IPTV Add-on on Kodi: Step-by-Step Guide (2026)

This section will guide you through installing an IPTV add-on on your Kodi system. Please remember that the availability and legality of IPTV add-ons can vary depending on your location. Always ensure you are complying with local laws and regulations. This guide assumes you already have Kodi installed and running.

### Step 1: Enable Unknown Sources

To install add-ons from outside the official Kodi repository, you need to enable installation from "Unknown sources."

1.  Open Kodi.
2.  Go to "Settings" (the gear icon).
3.  Click on "System."
4.  In the left-hand menu, select "Add-ons."
5.  Toggle the "Unknown sources" option to the "On" position.
6.  You will receive a warning message. Read it carefully and click "Yes" to proceed if you understand and accept the risks.

### Step 2: Add the Add-on Source

Next, you need to add the source URL for the IPTV add-on you want to install. This URL is usually provided by the add-on developer.

1.  Go back to the "System" menu (as in Step 1).
2.  Click on "File manager."
3.  Double-click on "Add source."
4.  Click on "<None>" and enter the exact URL of the repository containing the IPTV add-on. Double-check for typos!
5.  Give the source a name (e.g., "MyIPTVRepo") and click "OK."

### Step 3: Install the Add-on from the Repository

Now, you can install the add-on from the repository you just added.

1.  Go back to the main menu and click on "Add-ons."
2.  Click on the "Package installer" icon (looks like an open box) in the top-left corner.
3.  Choose "Install from zip file."
4.  Select the source you named in Step 2 (e.g., "MyIPTVRepo").
5.  Navigate to the zip file within the source, if there are multiple.
6.  Click on the zip file to install the repository.
7.  Once the repository is installed, select "Install from repository."
8.  Choose the repository you just installed.
9.  Go to "Video add-ons."
10. Find the IPTV add-on you want to install and click on it.
11. Click "Install."

The add-on will now install. Once complete, you will find it in your "Video add-ons" section. You may need to configure the add-on with your IPTV provider details.
