Who we are

BestIPTVTuto was created because finding honest, detailed information about IPTV services in the UK was genuinely difficult. Most review sites either copied information from provider websites without testing anything, or buried real opinions under affiliate disclaimers that made it unclear whether the reviewer had actually used the service.

We started this blog to fix that. Every service we cover is purchased with our own money, tested on real UK broadband connections across real devices, and reviewed according to a fixed scoring methodology that does not change based on who is paying for advertising.

We are not an IPTV provider. We do not sell subscriptions. We do not earn money when you sign up to a service unless you click an affiliate link, which we always disclose clearly. Our revenue comes from advertising and affiliate commissions — never from providers paying for better scores.

How we test IPTV services

Every IPTV subscription reviewed on BestIPTVTuto goes through the same process before we publish anything:

  • We buy the subscription ourselves. No free access, no sponsored plans. We pay the same price any member of the public would pay.
  • We test for a minimum of 21 days. Short tests miss the problems that only appear during high-traffic periods, after server changes, or during live sports events.
  • We test at peak hours. The 7 PM to 10 PM window on weekday evenings and Saturday afternoon during live sport are the hardest conditions for any IPTV service. We test specifically during these times.
  • We test on multiple devices. Every review covers at least Fire Stick, Smart TV, and a desktop setup. Device-specific issues are common and we report them honestly.
  • We test on multiple UK ISPs. Performance can vary significantly between BT, Sky, Virgin Media, and smaller providers. Where possible, we note ISP-specific behaviour.
  • We contact customer support. We submit a real support query and report the actual response time and quality of the answer.

Scores are never negotiated. Providers cannot pay to improve their score, request a re-test, or remove negative findings. If a service deteriorates after publication, we update the score and note the date of the update.

How we score IPTV services

Every service is scored across eight fixed categories. Each category is scored out of 10. The overall score is a weighted average.

Channel count

Total verified live channels and the quality of the UK channel lineup specifically.

UK IPTV quality

Picture quality on UK channels including HD/FHD/4K availability and consistency.

Stream stability

Buffering frequency and crash rate measured during peak UK viewing hours.

EPG accuracy

How much of the channel list has accurate programme guide data and how far ahead it shows.

IPTV plan value

Price per month relative to channel count, features, and competing services.

Customer support

Availability, response time, and quality of support measured by a real test query.

Device compatibility

How many devices are supported and how well the service performs on each.

Ease of setup

How straightforward the initial installation and configuration process is.

Editorial policy

BestIPTVTuto accepts advertising and participates in affiliate programmes. This means some articles may contain affiliate links, and some content may be sponsored by IPTV providers. Our policy on this is straightforward:

  • Sponsored content is always disclosed at the top of the article. If a provider has paid for coverage, you will see a clear disclosure before the article begins.
  • Affiliate links do not affect scores. A service with an affiliate programme does not receive a higher score than one without. We have declined affiliate partnerships with services that did not meet our standards.
  • We do not accept payment for review scores. Providers cannot pay to improve, maintain, or remove a score. Scores reflect our independent testing.
  • We update reviews when services change. If a service significantly improves or deteriorates after publication, we re-test and update the score, noting the date of the update.
